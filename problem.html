The Problem
"I want to be a doctor when I grow up, but my family cannot afford to send me to school."

— A 10-year-old girl in rural Pakistan, via UNICEF field report

Barriers to Education

💰

Poverty

For millions of families across South Asia, the cost of school fees, uniforms, and supplies is simply too high. When families struggle to put food on the table, education becomes a luxury they cannot afford.

Nearly 1 in 5 children in South Asia live in extreme poverty
🏫

Lack of Schools & Teachers

In many rural areas, the nearest school can be hours away. Even where schools exist, there are often not enough trained teachers or proper facilities to provide a quality education.

South Asia needs over 2 million more trained teachers
⚕️

Gender Inequality

Girls are disproportionately affected. Cultural norms, early marriage, and safety concerns keep millions of girls out of the classroom. In some areas, girls are twice as likely as boys to never attend school.

Over 12 million girls in South Asia are out of school
⛏

Child Labor

Millions of children work instead of learning — in fields, factories, and homes. When families depend on a child's income to survive, school is no longer an option.

South Asia has the highest number of child laborers globally — over 26 million
The Consequences

What Happens When Kids Can't Go to School?

When children are denied an education, the consequences go far beyond the classroom. Without the skills and opportunities that school provides, many children end up trapped in a cycle that is incredibly hard to escape.

Children who can't go to school often end up on the streets, where survival becomes their only priority. Some turn to stealing just to eat. When they get caught, they enter the justice system — and many end up in jail. Without education, they have no path to a job, no way to support themselves, and no way out.

This isn't just a problem for those children — it affects entire communities. Education breaks the cycle of poverty and crime. When we invest in a child's education, we invest in a safer, stronger future for everyone.
